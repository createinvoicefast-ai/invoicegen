Invoicing Guide

Simple invoicing practices that make your invoices easier to approve and faster to pay.

1. Keep invoice details complete

Include a clear invoice number, issue date, due date, and client details. Missing basics create delays.

2. Use clear line items

Each row should explain what was delivered. Keep descriptions specific and quantity/rate values easy to verify.

3. Make totals obvious

Show tax, discounts, shipping, total, and balance due in one area. Clients should not guess the final amount.

4. Add payment terms

Write terms in plain language. Include accepted payment methods and any late fee policy if needed.

5. Save and reuse

Save invoices to your account history so you can duplicate patterns, track payment status, and reduce data entry errors.
